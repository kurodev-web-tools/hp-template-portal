# Portfolio Category Design Guide

## Purpose
Portfolio templates are designed to showcase work, perspective, and authorship while helping viewers understand quality and fit.

## Shared Visual Direction
- Work-first, visually intentional, composed
- The site should feel like an extension of the creator or studio
- More expressive than business templates, but still curated

## Layout Rules
- Give work room to breathe
- Use composition to control pacing and emphasis
- Prefer layouts that let projects, case studies, and visuals lead the experience

## Typography
- Typography may carry more personality here than in other categories
- Readability still matters, but controlled character is welcome
- Use hierarchy to frame the work, not overpower it

## Color & Surface
- Color should either support the work quietly or become part of the creator's identity
- Avoid palettes that compete with portfolio pieces unless the concept is intentionally bold

## Motion
- Motion can be used for mood, transitions, and reveal
- It should enhance presentation without stealing attention from the work

## CTA & Interaction
- CTA is secondary to the work, but still needs clear access points
- Contact, profile, and case-study navigation should feel intentional and elegant

## Anti-Patterns
- Generic gallery layouts with no pacing
- Decorative systems that overpower the work
- Reusing the same project-grid logic across multiple templates

## Differentiation Guidance
- Different portfolio templates should vary by whitespace philosophy, project sequencing, storytelling structure, and visual framing
- Each template should have a distinct relationship between typography, imagery, and layout rhythm
