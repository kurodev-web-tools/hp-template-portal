# Business Category Design Guide

## Purpose
Business templates are designed for companies, studios, and professional services that need to communicate trust, clarity, and conversion intent.

## Shared Visual Direction
- Calm, credible, polished
- Structured rather than playful
- Professional without feeling generic or lifeless

## Layout Rules
- Prioritize clarity and information hierarchy over spectacle
- The first viewport should explain the value of the business quickly
- Prefer trust-building flow: overview, proof, services, CTA

## Typography
- Headings should feel confident and readable
- Body text should optimize for clarity and scanability
- Avoid novelty-first display typography unless the concept is explicitly premium

## Color & Surface
- Use a controlled palette with one clear accent
- Maintain strong contrast for key information
- Accent colors should support CTA and structure, not dominate the whole page

## Motion
- Motion should feel deliberate and restrained
- Prefer fade, reveal, slight slide, and small stagger
- Avoid playful or overly expressive motion systems

## CTA & Interaction
- CTA should appear early and repeat with intent
- Contact and inquiry flows should feel trustworthy and low-friction

## Anti-Patterns
- Overly playful composition
- Decorative gradients with no informational role
- Motion that competes with messaging

## Differentiation Guidance
- Different templates should vary by rhythm, density, proof presentation, and hero composition
- Do not reuse the same section order or animation pattern across multiple business templates
