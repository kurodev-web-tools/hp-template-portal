# LP Category Design Guide

## Purpose
LP templates are designed to convert a visitor around a single offer, campaign, or value proposition with minimal distraction.

## Shared Visual Direction
- Clear, persuasive, high-contrast
- Focused on momentum and action
- Stronger and more direct than business templates

## Layout Rules
- The hero should communicate the offer, audience, and CTA immediately
- Section order should reinforce persuasion: value, proof, objection handling, CTA
- Keep navigation and side paths minimal unless necessary

## Typography
- Headlines carry a large share of the persuasion work
- Strong visual contrast between headline, support copy, and CTA
- Body text should stay concise and benefit-oriented

## Color & Surface
- Color should create emphasis and urgency without becoming noisy
- CTA styling must be unmistakable
- Background shifts and section contrast can be used to maintain pace

## Motion
- Motion should support attention and visual sequencing
- Use it to stage reveals, highlight proof, and reinforce CTA
- Avoid ornamental animation that slows decision-making

## CTA & Interaction
- CTA should be obvious, repeated, and easy to act on
- Form or conversion friction should be as low as possible

## Anti-Patterns
- Dense informational layouts
- Weak CTA contrast
- Overcomplicated navigation or too many competing actions

## Differentiation Guidance
- Different LP templates should vary by hero structure, persuasion rhythm, proof style, and CTA cadence
- Do not make multiple LP templates feel like the same wireframe with color swaps
