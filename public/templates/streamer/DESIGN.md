# Streamer Category Design Guide

## Purpose
Streamer templates are designed to express personality, strengthen fan connection, and guide visitors toward streams, schedules, and social channels.

## Shared Visual Direction
- Energetic, character-driven, memorable
- More playful and expressive than other categories
- Built around identity and audience connection

## Layout Rules
- Put character, stream identity, and audience entry points near the top
- Social, schedule, clips, and fan actions should be easy to find
- The layout may be more decorative, but it still needs clear navigation

## Typography
- Typography can be bold, playful, or stylized when it supports the persona
- Headings should carry energy and recognizability
- Body text should remain readable even when the visual language is expressive

## Color & Surface
- Lean into character colors, thematic palettes, and mood systems
- Visual energy is welcome, but palette choices should still feel intentional
- Decorative surfaces and layered backgrounds are allowed when they reinforce identity

## Motion
- Stronger motion is acceptable here than in other categories
- Use motion to create energy, reveal highlights, and reinforce personality
- Avoid chaos: motion should still feel directed and readable

## CTA & Interaction
- Prioritize stream links, social follows, schedules, and community actions
- Interactive elements should feel lively and responsive

## Anti-Patterns
- Flat layouts with no personality
- Generic creator pages with only color swaps
- Decorative overload that harms readability or navigation

## Differentiation Guidance
- Different streamer templates should vary by energy level, graphic density, mascot or identity treatment, and social or community emphasis
- Do not reuse the same hero composition, schedule block, or motion pattern across multiple streamer templates
